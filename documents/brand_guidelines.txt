BRAND GUIDELINES: Apex Digital Agency

Document Version: 3.0
Last Updated: January 2025
Approved By: Sarah Chen, Chief Brand Officer

1. BRAND OVERVIEW

Apex Digital Agency is a full-service digital advertising agency specializing in data-driven creative campaigns for enterprise clients. Founded in 2015, we serve Fortune 500 companies across technology, finance, healthcare, and consumer goods sectors.

Mission: To bridge the gap between data intelligence and creative excellence, delivering campaigns that are both analytically sound and emotionally compelling.

Vision: To be the world's most trusted agency for AI-powered advertising solutions by 2030.

2. BRAND VOICE & TONE

Our brand voice is:
- Confident but not arrogant: We lead with expertise, not ego
- Innovative but accessible: We make complex technology feel approachable
- Data-informed but human: Numbers guide us, but stories drive us
- Professional but warm: We're serious about our work, not about ourselves

TONE GUIDELINES BY CONTEXT:
- Client Presentations: Authoritative, polished, insight-led
- Social Media: Conversational, witty, thought-provoking
- Case Studies: Results-focused, narrative-driven, specific
- Internal Communications: Transparent, collaborative, encouraging
- Crisis Communications: Empathetic, factual, solution-oriented

WORDS WE USE: Insight, Transform, Amplify, Precision, Impact, Collaborate, Innovate, Empower
WORDS WE AVOID: Disrupt, Crush it, Game-changer, Guru, Ninja, Hack, Pivot, Synergy

3. VISUAL IDENTITY

PRIMARY COLORS:
- Apex Navy: #1B2A4A (primary brand color — used for headers, CTAs, key elements)
- Apex Coral: #FF6B5B (accent color — used for highlights, data visualizations, CTAs)
- Pure White: #FFFFFF (background, clean space)

SECONDARY COLORS:
- Soft Gray: #F5F5F5 (backgrounds, cards)
- Charcoal: #333333 (body text)
- Mint: #4ECDC4 (success states, positive data)
- Amber: #FFB347 (warning states, attention)

TYPOGRAPHY:
- Headlines: Inter Bold (32-48px)
- Subheads: Inter SemiBold (20-28px)
- Body Copy: Inter Regular (16px, line-height 1.6)
- Captions: Inter Light (14px)
- Code/Data: JetBrains Mono (14px)

LOGO USAGE:
- Minimum clear space: 2x the height of the "A" in Apex
- Minimum size: 120px width for digital, 1 inch for print
- Never stretch, rotate, or alter the logo proportions
- Approved backgrounds: White, Navy, or photography with sufficient contrast
- Never place the logo on busy backgrounds without a container

4. PHOTOGRAPHY & IMAGE GUIDELINES

Style: Modern, clean, authentic — no stock photography cliches
People: Diverse representation in age, ethnicity, gender, and ability
Settings: Real workplaces, genuine moments, natural lighting preferred
Technology: Show technology as a tool, not the hero — focus on human outcomes
Avoid: Overly staged poses, handshake photos, generic office scenes, cliched "team high-five" shots

5. SOCIAL MEDIA GUIDELINES

Platform-specific rules:
- LinkedIn: Thought leadership, case studies, industry insights (post 3-5x/week)
- Instagram: Behind-the-scenes, team culture, visual case studies (post 4-6x/week)
- Twitter/X: Industry commentary, quick insights, engagement with community (post 5-10x/week)
- TikTok: Creative process videos, trend participation, educational content (post 2-3x/week)

Hashtag Strategy:
- Always include: #ApexDigital #DataDrivenCreative
- Campaign-specific hashtags should be pre-approved by the marketing team
- Maximum 5 hashtags per post on Instagram, 2-3 on LinkedIn/Twitter

6. CONTENT APPROVAL PROCESS

All external-facing content must go through:
1. Creator drafts content
2. Team Lead reviews for accuracy and tone
3. Brand Manager approves visual and voice compliance
4. Legal review (required for claims, statistics, client mentions)
5. Final sign-off by Department Head

Turnaround times:
- Social media posts: 24-hour approval cycle
- Blog posts/articles: 48-hour approval cycle
- Case studies: 5-business-day approval cycle
- Client-facing presentations: 3-business-day approval cycle

7. CLIENT CONFIDENTIALITY

- Never share client data, budgets, or internal metrics without written approval
- Case studies require explicit client sign-off before publication
- Remove all client-identifiable information from internal training materials
- NDA terms must be reviewed before any public mention of client relationships
