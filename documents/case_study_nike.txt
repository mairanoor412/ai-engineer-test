CASE STUDY: Nike "Just Do It" 35th Anniversary Campaign

Client: Nike, Inc.
Agency: Wieden+Kennedy Portland
Campaign Duration: September 2023 - December 2023
Budget: $50 Million

OBJECTIVE:
Celebrate the 35th anniversary of Nike's iconic "Just Do It" slogan while connecting with a new generation of athletes and fitness enthusiasts. The campaign aimed to increase brand relevance among Gen-Z consumers (ages 16-24) while maintaining loyalty among existing customers.

TARGET AUDIENCE:
Primary: Gen-Z athletes and fitness enthusiasts (16-24 years old)
Secondary: Millennial fitness community (25-35 years old)
Psychographics: Values authenticity, social justice, self-expression, and community

STRATEGY:
The campaign used a multi-platform approach centered around real stories from everyday athletes. Instead of relying solely on celebrity endorsements, Nike featured local community athletes, coaches, and fitness influencers who embodied the "Just Do It" spirit in their daily lives.

Key tactical elements:
1. Hero Film: A 90-second anthem spot featuring diverse athletes from 12 countries
2. Social Series: 15 short-form videos (30-60 seconds) for TikTok and Instagram Reels
3. Interactive Experience: AR try-on feature in the Nike app
4. Community Events: Pop-up fitness events in 8 major cities
5. Influencer Program: Partnered with 200+ micro-influencers (10K-100K followers)

CHANNELS:
- Television (national broadcast + streaming platforms)
- Social Media (TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Twitter/X)
- Digital Display (programmatic + premium placements)
- Out-of-Home (billboards in 15 major markets)
- Nike App (exclusive content + AR features)
- Retail (in-store displays + limited edition packaging)

CREATIVE APPROACH:
Tone: Empowering, inclusive, authentic, raw
Visual Style: Documentary-style, natural lighting, diverse casting
Music: Original score blending hip-hop and electronic elements
Tagline Extension: "Just Do It. Your Way."

RESULTS:
- Brand awareness increased 12% among Gen-Z demographic
- Social media engagement up 340% during campaign period
- Nike app downloads increased 28% in Q4 2023
- TikTok campaign hashtag generated 2.1 billion views
- Sales lift of 15% in Direct-to-Consumer channel
- Earned media value: $120 million
- Campaign sentiment: 89% positive across social platforms

KEY LEARNINGS:
1. Micro-influencers delivered 3x higher engagement rates than celebrity partnerships
2. TikTok performed best for Gen-Z reach; Instagram for Millennial engagement
3. AR experiences drove 45% longer app session times
4. Community events generated the highest quality user-generated content
5. Documentary-style content outperformed polished commercial content by 2:1

BUDGET BREAKDOWN:
- Media Buying: 40% ($20M)
- Content Production: 25% ($12.5M)
- Influencer Partnerships: 15% ($7.5M)
- Technology/AR Development: 10% ($5M)
- Events & Activations: 10% ($5M)
